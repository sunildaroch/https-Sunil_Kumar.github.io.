About

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow at AGH University of Science and Technology in Krakow, Poland, specializing in Discrete Element Method (DEM) simulations. My research focuses on lunar soil excavation mechanics, granular flow behavior, particle segregation, and mixing phenomena under various conditions including microgravity.

I completed my Ph.D. in Particle Technology from IIT Roorkee with a CGPA of 8.4/10.0, where I developed expertise in numerical simulations, experimental validation, and computational modeling of granular systems.

  • Research Focus: Lunar regolith mechanics, DEM simulations, granular mixing & segregation, particle technology
  • Languages: Python, C/C++, Bash, LaTeX
  • Simulation Software: LIGGGHTS, Rocky, EDEM, Yade, OpenFOAM
  • Expertise: Discrete Element Method (DEM), Experimental validation, Parameter calibration, Statistical analysis (ANOVA, MCMC)

Looking forward to pursuing a faculty position at leading Indian research institutes (IIT/NIT) where I can combine my expertise in DEM simulations with teaching and mentoring the next generation of researchers.
